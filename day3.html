Morning


Explore the fashionable Place du Grand Sablon and the area's must see, the Notre Dame du Sablon.

Place du Grand Sablon


Situated between the divisions of the Upper and Lower town in Brussels, it is like a stepping stone between the Upper and Lower towns. The mean ing "sablon" in French means sand and the square is so-called because this old route was passed through an area of sandy marshes. However, the modern picture is very different as the area is surrounded by elegant townhouses, some with the Art Nouveau facades. This is a very chic, wealthy and antique place where fashionable restaurants, bars and chocolate shops lie.

Notre Dame du Sablon


Notre Dame du Sablon is very similar to the Cathedral Sts Michel et Gudule, it is one of the Brabant Gothic architecture in Belgium. The church was first built on a legend stating that a girl has seen Virgin Mary who instructed her to take her statue to Brussels. Therefore, at the entrance we can see two carvings stating the myth after the statue was destroyed in 1565. The interior of the building is simple but beautifully proportioned. It contains 14 m high glasses, and lights shine out at night like welcoming beacons.

Afternoon


Head out on the metro to visit Heysel to see the striking Atomium. But do not miss the Mini-Europe exhibition near Bruparck. You can also spend more time in the Domaine de Laeken.

The Atomium

This most identifiable symbols of Brussels was designed by Anfre Waterkeyn to commemorate the new age of science and space travel. It is formed of iron crystals magnified 165 billion times. In which each of the "atom" are 18 m in diameter and linked by escalators and stairs. Inside, there are exhibition rooms and tiny restaurant.

Bruparck

This region is a very family enjoyable destination where more than 300 miniature reconstruction is around the European Union. It shows a great cultural importance such as the Acropolis in Athens and the Brandenburg Gate of Berlin.

Domaine de Laeken

The Domaine de Laeken is the home to royals since the 19 th centuries and a walk in here would present impressive buildings constructed for the royal location. But the most impressive are the building that the great builder, King Leopold II's, an architectural world tour which includes the Chinese towers and many others.
